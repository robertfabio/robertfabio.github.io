    Lazy machine learning algorithms have to learn every time it is been given a new test example, however knowing which example is being classified give them the advantage to adjust their knowledge search accordingly. Lazy Associative Classifier (LAC) is a rule-based demand-driven lazy machine learning algorithm that takes advantage of that information by focusing its effort on inducing rules around the region that the test example is situated. Rule-based algorithms are often caught in the dilemma of not knowing the best way to combine their rules in order to form the best possible model. Usually, a choosing of a rule metric followed by a simple voting is used (as simple as assigning an importance – or weight – of one to each rule and averaging the accounts by each class). This approach is easily proven to be frail. In this work we use the Adaboost algorithm in conjunction of LAC to compare the accuracy o fixed and personalized rule weights and show the superiority of our approach.
